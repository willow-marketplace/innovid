## Understanding Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (gen AI) is a distinct category of AI that leverages large language models (LLMs) to generate new, original content and other assets. Gen AI's generative capabilities & ability to perform a range of complex tasks sets it apart from traditional machine learning.

The most common use of generative AI is when creating branded chatbots, developing graphics and visual illustrations, and generating text-based content informed by data repositories containing proprietary information. But today's businesses have only scratched the surface of generative AI's potential use cases. This powerful generative technology offers businesses a number of key benefits, including:

### Better Resource Allocation

Generative AI can automate a wide range of menial tasks that no longer require human labor. By automating these workloads and reassigning human workers to focus on more complicated tasks, businesses can optimize their internal resource allocation and [increase their overall productivity](/startups/learn/generative-ai-the-next-productivity-frontier).

### Accelerate Product Development

Many startups use test-and-iterate development models to create and refine new product offerings. Generative AI accelerates these development cycles, enabling organizations to scale up product experimentation and testing.

Founders can use generative AI to explore a wider range of product possibilities, iterate through ideas with unprecedented speed, and develop better, more innovative products faster than ever before.

### Cost Reduction

By accelerating coding, testing, and other key creative and development processes, [startups should be able to cut costs](/startups/learn/how-startups-lower-ai-ml-costs-and-innovate-with-aws-inferentia) across a wide range of business activities.

This combination of cost-efficiencies with performance gains is similar to how Amazon Web Services reduced computing, storage, and networking costs for startups.

## Evaluating Generative AI Learning Resources

There's no shortage of generative AI learning resources out there—and that can make it difficult to identify the ones that best serve your interests.

As you wade through your options, here are some tips to help you evaluate these resources:

### Relevance to Business Needs

Does the learning resource align with your specific business and use case needs? If there's not a clear relevance or benefit, it's probably not the right fit.

### Check Reputation and Reviews

Nobody wants to waste time on a learning course that doesn't offer a great education. Before you enroll, evaluate the credibility and quality of the course by reading online reviews of the course, the instructor, and the platform or institution where the course is being offered.

These reviews can tell you a little more about what the resource offers, and whether it's worth your time.

### Use Hands-On Learning

Prioritize resources that offer hands-on learning such as labs, projects, and real-world use cases. This practical experience will accelerate your mastery of important skills such as prompt engineering and fine-tuning generative models.

### Consider Learning Schedule Flexibility

Some learning resources let you go at your own pace, but others have strict schedules—especially if collaboration and partner work is involved. Make sure the resource's schedule can accommodate your own schedule and your preferred pace of learning.

### Adequate Support

Check each learning resource for available support channels such as forums, help desks, and direct communication with instructors. You don't want to be in a situation where platform issues and other technical problems disrupt your learning experience.
